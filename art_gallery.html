  • 1st art(Mona Lisa)

      • The Mona Lisa is an iconic Renaissance portrait painting by Leonardo da Vinci, created between 1503 and 1519, depicting Lisa del Giocondo. Housed in the Louvre Museum in Paris, it is famous for the subject's enigmatic expression and has become the most visited and parodied artwork globally. The painting's fame was significantly boosted after its 1911 theft, which captured headlines and intensified public interest upon its return

    • 2nd art(Annapurna by Nandalal Bose)

      • Nandalal Bose, a leading figure in contemporary Indian art, showcased "Annapurna" - a renowned portrayal of the goddess of nourishment to the world. Unlike traditional depictions of deities, Bose's Annapurna is distinguished by a kind, nurturing, and straightforward nature. The painting portrays the goddess with a simple and elegant style, showing her as both powerful and graceful, emphasizing her role as the divine provider. The natural colors and elegant brushwork clearly show Bose's commitment to keeping Indian art connected to its tradition while also allowing for growth.

The serene figure of Mother India, holding items like a book, grain, and cloth, represents the spirit of self-reliance.
